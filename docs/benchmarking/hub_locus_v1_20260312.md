## Analysis & Insights

### 1. Gain Scheduling vs Static Tikhonov: Different Optimal Points

The two Tikhonov variants optimize for different things:

- **Static α = 0.25** wins on reprojection RMSE across all resolutions (−3–6% vs baseline).
  Uniformly flooring corner uncertainty slightly smooths the Mahalanobis loss and reduces
  reprojection scatter even for well-conditioned frontal tags.

- **Gain-scheduled α(R) = 0.25·(1−R)²** wins on the translation tail at high resolutions.
  1080p Trans P99 drops from 0.562 m (baseline) to **0.437 m (−22%)** — the grazing-angle
  outlier case the static version actively worsened (0.595 m). This is the primary design
  goal: preserve precision for frontal tags, bound the observer gain at grazing angles.

Neither is universally dominant; the optimal choice depends on application:
- **High-precision metrology / close range**: static α wins (better reprojection RMSE).
- **Robustness under variable pose geometry / long range**: gain-scheduled wins (P99 tail).

### 2. Trans P90 Close-Range Regression (480p)

Both Tikhonov variants regress Trans P90 at 480p (0.030 m and 0.026 m vs 0.022 m baseline).
The gain scheduler is worse because even at moderate anisotropy R ≈ 0.5 it applies α ≈ 0.06,
suppressing some valid gradient information. The 480p regime is precision-sensitive enough to
feel any information loss.

### 3. Rotation Tail is Determined by IPPE Initialization

P90/P99 rotation errors at 1080p (32.6° / 74.5°) are unchanged across all Tikhonov variants
and both refinement modes. The failures are IPPE disambiguation errors (selecting the wrong
of the two IPPE solutions on near-head-on tags). No covariance shaping in the LM refinement
step can escape a wrong initial basin. Fixing the rotation tail requires improving the IPPE
candidate selection criterion.

### 4. Accurate Mode Retains Strong P99 Translation Lead at 1080p

With gain-scheduled Tikhonov, Accurate mode P99 at 1080p (0.437 m) leads Fast (0.623 m)
by **30%**. The anisotropic structure-tensor covariance, modulated by the gain schedule,
provides directional uncertainty information that isotropic Fast mode cannot replicate at
extreme grazing angles.

### 5. Detection is Unaffected

Recall, precision, and Det. RMSE are identical between modes — upstream of pose estimation.

## Methodology
- **Data Source:** Hugging Face `single_tag_locus_v1_tag36h11` subsets (45–50 images each).
- **Harness:** `locus-core` regression suite (`regression_render_tag`), Erf corner refinement.
  Snapshots: `regression_render_tag__hub_*_{fast,}.snap`
- **Environment:** Linux, Release Profile (`--release`).
- **Commit:** See git log for exact solver version used.
