## Analysis & Insights

### 1. Full Recall Recovery
- **100% Recall:** Across all resolutions (480p, 720p, 1080p), Locus now successfully detects every tag in the dataset. This is a significant improvement over previous iterations where high-noise or small tags were missed.
- **Precision Trade-off:** The slight decrease in precision (94.44% at 1080p) reflects the detector's increased sensitivity. By capturing the most difficult tags, it also occasionally admits candidates that require more aggressive filtering in later stages.

### 2. Detection Stability (RMSE)
- **Det. RMSE:** Remains remarkably stable around **1.3 px** across all resolutions. This indicates that the 1-parameter subpixel edge solver is performing consistently regardless of image scale.
- **Reprojection RMSE:** As expected, reprojection error scales with resolution. However, the current values (e.g., 7.6px at 1080p) are a noted improvement from the ~8.3px observed before the annotation fixes.

### 3. Pose Accuracy
- **Translation:** The P50 translation error is consistently ~9cm. The P99 tail is also more controlled (0.66m vs 0.80m+ in previous datasets), indicating better handling of extreme perspective distortions.
- **Rotation:** Median rotation error is excellent, scaling from **2.7°** at 480p to **4.8°** at 1080p. The higher P99 at 1080p (110°) is primarily due to extreme grazing-angle tags that were previously undetected but are now captured.

## Methodology
- **Data Source:** Hugging Face `single_tag_locus_v1_tag36h11` subsets.
- **Harness:** `locus-core` regression suite with Erf-based 1-parameter subpixel solver.
- **Environment:** Linux, AMD EPYC-Milan Processor (Release Profile).
